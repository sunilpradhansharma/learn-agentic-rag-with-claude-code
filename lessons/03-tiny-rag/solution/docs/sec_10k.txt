A Form 10-K is an annual report that publicly traded companies in the United States must file with the Securities and Exchange Commission. It provides a comprehensive overview of the company's financial condition, including audited financial statements, a description of the business, risk factors, and management's discussion and analysis of results. The 10-K is more detailed than a company's annual report to shareholders and must be filed within 60 to 90 days after the end of the fiscal year, depending on the company's size. The filing is a primary source of information for investors, analysts, and regulators assessing the long-term health of a business.
