A Form 10-Q is a quarterly report that U.S. public companies file with the SEC for each of the first three quarters of their fiscal year. It contains unaudited financial statements and updates on material events since the most recent annual report, including any significant changes in operations, liquidity, or risk factors. The 10-Q must be filed within 40 to 45 days after the end of each fiscal quarter, depending on company size. Because it is filed three times per year and is unaudited, the 10-Q provides a more frequent but less formally verified picture of a company's financial condition than the annual 10-K.
