The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is the primary federal regulatory agency responsible for enforcing federal securities laws and regulating the securities industry in the United States. It was established by the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, following the 1929 stock market crash, to restore investor confidence through disclosure requirements and anti-fraud provisions. The SEC requires public companies to file regular financial reports — including 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K forms — which are made publicly available through the EDGAR database at sec.gov. The agency also oversees securities exchanges, broker-dealers, investment advisers, and mutual funds.
