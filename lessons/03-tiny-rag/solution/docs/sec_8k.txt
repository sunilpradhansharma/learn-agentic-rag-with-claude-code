A Form 8-K is a current report that public companies must file with the SEC to announce material events that shareholders and the investing public should know about promptly. Triggering events include mergers and acquisitions, departures or appointments of executives, amendments to articles of incorporation, bankruptcy filings, and changes in fiscal year. Companies are generally required to file an 8-K within four business days of the triggering event. Because 8-K filings are time-sensitive and event-driven, they are closely watched by investors and analysts as early signals of significant corporate changes.
